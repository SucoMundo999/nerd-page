What to Expect from "Starfield" When diving into the world of "Starfield", it's essential to first consider the lineage from which it emerges. Bethesda, the powerhouse behind it, has given us epic titles like "Fallout" and "The Elder Scrolls" – both arguably some of the biggest game series in history. It's evident that traces of these predecessors will seep into the DNA of "Starfield".

From the glimpses we've seen in game previews, "Fallout" seems to have lent its inspiration especially in the department of weaponry and attire. Players are sure to notice familiar aesthetics in both the weapons and the attire their characters don.

However, the heart of the buzz surrounds the game's role-playing elements. Fans are eagerly anticipating a storyline that's nothing short of legendary, holding it up against Bethesda's previous masterpieces. "Starfield" promises a web of factions, like the United Colonies and the Freestar Collective, offering players a rich tapestry of interactions and relationships to delve into.

A significant feature that has piqued the interest of many is the innovative spaceship creation and navigation system. Players can design their spacecraft and voyage across the cosmos, capturing the essence of a true space exploration game.

Yet, not all is stardust and smooth travels. There's a looming question: Will "Starfield" be well-optimized? Memories of "Fallout 76" with its infamous bugs, performance issues, and underwhelming frame rates still haunt many players. However, it's worth noting that "Fallout 76" marked Bethesda's maiden foray into the multiplayer realm, which "Starfield" won't be venturing into. Thus, there's hope that without the complexities of multiplayer servers, "Starfield" might shine brighter.

But, as with all upcoming releases, there's an element of uncertainty. Especially when the price tag reads $70, gamers rightfully expect a polished gem. Only time will tell if "Starfield" lives up to the cosmic expectations set for it.
